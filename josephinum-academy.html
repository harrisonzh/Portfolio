Josephinum Academy

Role: UX Researcher and UI Designer

Duration: Jan 25, 2019 - May 20, 2019 (16 weeks)

Team: Research and design with a 4-person team

Project Description

Josephinum Academy of the Sacred Heart is a Roman Catholic, all-girls high school in Chicago, Illinois. The owner not satisfied with their current website, they want to offer a more professional website and focus on donating and apply. Designed for an Advanced UX Research class, Spring 2019.

My Role

My role in the team is a UX researcher and a UI designer. I made the wireframe of the Homepage, Student life page, Admissions page, and About Us page. I analyzed other school websites and conducted a card sort survey with our target group to help we design the Josephinum website. I built the donor persona Fiona, I drew Storyboard based on the persona Jo’s family. I lead two usability tests to make sure the solution represent by our design is useful and appealing.

Goals

Target Audience

The audience for Josephinum Academy website is prospective families and prospective donors.

Prospective Family

The primary audience is prospective families in the Chicago area with kids in middle school. Priorities are to have their daughter attend a high school with great academics and activities while still being affordable. They want to prepare their daughter for college. This can be an exciting time for prospective families but also a bit overwhelming. They are often looking for specific things for their daughter to participate in (i.e. debate club, basketball, swimming).

Prospective Donors

Prospective donors are Chicago-based private donors, corporate and mission-aligned nonprofits. The donors have a connection to Catholic education, girls' education/empowerment, improved educational opportunities for inner city/low-income families, Latina community support, African American community support, and IB education. They are looking to further their contribution in these areas by donating to Josephinum. The donor needs to know why they should donate, what the donation goes towards, the values and mission of the school, verification that the school received the donation, and what specific project their money is going towards.

Competitor Analysis

To begin our competitive analysis, we researched four high schools in the Chicago area to find websites that would be reasonable to compare to Josephinum. After reviewing the four websites, we created a structured list of questions to find how those websites perform as a high school website. We considered calls to action, photography, donation pages, mobile optimization and how the school conveys their brand. After we conducted the competitive analyses, we have come to the conclusion that the competitors have a few advancements in their websites that we agree would be a good idea to emulate when redesigning the Josephinum website. Features and Functions

The application and shadowing/visiting scenario will be essential to the experience because of the client’s goal. The home page will also need to show why josephinum is so special and a brief info section about them. This info section will include key facts, statistics, and values that represent the quality of the school but will find a balance between necessary knowledge and more detailed descriptions. From the home page will be direct links to apply and donate, as these are the desired destinations for our core audiences. Users should be able to easily navigate to the application page and/or donation page, and quickly and effortlessly complete each process respectively. The user will complete a form (that should be intuitive and easily understood) for both the application page and donation page. After submitting the form, a confirmation page will appear to indicate the completion of the process.

Open Card Sort

Our team conducted an open card sort study using OptimalSort. We gave participants the task of organizing 37 “cards” each containing a title from the current Josephinum website navigation into category names that made sense. The final study included responses from 41 participants, 16 of them being parents. The purpose of this test was to evaluate how users group the content of the Josephinum website and in what categories.
