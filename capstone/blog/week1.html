Project Description

LandmarkAR is an AR app to show the landmark architectures around world. Users can visit and learn about landmark architectures by using the app. People can place the small-scale landmark building on the ground, or place a full-scale building on an open space outdoor. People can even walk inside to visit the interior of the building.

Plan

For this week, I plan to identify target users and conduct user research on my target users.

Target users

  • People who like landmark architectures
  • People who are keen on traveling
  • People who are passionate about history

Goals

  1. Allows users to learn about the landmarks by using the app.
  2. Allows users to experience the environment by using the app.
  3. Learn about landmark architecture and related history through AR.
  4. Promote users' interest in these architectures.

User Research

After identify target user, I wrote a user reseach script and ask the following questions to my target users:

  1. What made your trip impossible?
  2. Have you ever wanted to visit an attraction/building, but failed to do so, what’s the reason behind that?
  3. Can you describe your experience of visiting ancient buildings/landmarks?
  4. Do you want to learn knowledge while visiting landmark buildings?
  5. If you want to visit a landmark, what is most important to you?
  6. What is most important to you during travel?
  7. What will move you when you visit the interior of a building?
  8. Have you used any AR/VR apps? How does it feel?
  9. Have you seen a building through AR/VR? How will you improve your experience?
  10. Would you like to visit a building through AR?

Research

Sample size: 10 participants in total. 3 of them are female, and 7 of them are male.

  • 5 participants for user segment A (People who are keen on traveling)
  • 3 participants for user segment B (People who like landmark architectures)
  • 2 participants for user segment B (People who are passionate about history)

Affinity Diagrams

I grouped our research findings using Affinity Diagrams.

Research Findings

during the interview, I note that my target users like to focus on details, enjoy the feeling in the environment. Participants said, "When I visit a temple, I feel immersed because I hear Buddhist music.", "In an ancient temple, I was very moved to see the part of the mural that had not fallen off. I feel like a time traveller.", "I remember that there was a window in the Humble Administrator's Garden in Suzhou, and all the sunlight came in from that window. It was beautiful, at the right time, and with the right light. It gave me a good experience.", "I love the sound of wind blowing in the woods, the sound of birds when I visit a historic landmark.", so a background music will provide users a better experience. My participants also said, "I always have a tour guide who tell me some stories behind the building", "I want to learn the story about the landmark", "I will read the introduction written on the sign of the tourist attraction.", "I like to learn the history behind the architecture", So it's important t have a introduction of the landmark building. I also got the following suggestions: "Music and sound affect is super important.", "Click on some parts of the building and play the voice introduction.", "Make the model accurate enough.", "See other people who are visiting the building in real time.", "Voice guide or Storytelling function", "Plots or scenarios, such as people in the Qing dynasty walking through the palace."

Key Findings

  • Users like to focus on details.
  • Lighting, sound, details create great memories.
  • When I visit a temple, I feel immersed because I hear Buddhist music.
  • Users like to learn the history and stories behind the architecture.
  • Music and sound affect is super important.
  • Users wants to hear the voice introduction of the landmark.
