Know Your Grove

Role: UX/UI Designer

Duration: Dec 1, 2019 - June 25, 2020 (7 months)

Team: Design with a 10-person team

Project Description

In order to display the landmark architectures in Miamiâ€™s historic district Coconut Grove, professor Kim Grinfeder led a team to collaborate with Andrew W. Mellon Foundation, University of Miami Library, and Lowe Art Museum to develop an APP which provide tourists and local residents with an interesting way to visit the famous historic district in downtown Miami: Coconut Grove.
Coconut Grove is a historic neighborhood with more than a century-old history. It is home to the most famous historical landmark in Miami: Vizcaya Museum and Gardens, Plymouth Congregational Church, the hundred-year-old big banyan tree and the peacocks are everywhere in Coconut Grove. The City of Miami government is also located here.
Know Your Grove will take tourists to visit the famous buildings in the area by cycling or walking and provide tourists and local residents with an interesting way to visit, bringing a different cultural journey to our users.

My Role

My role in the team is as a UX/UI designer. I conducted research on local residents, and used Adobe XD to complete the high-fidelity prototype design of the mobile app interface and the desktop version web interface. After the design is completed, I did a few user testing, and updated the prototype design based on user feedback. And then, I had a communication with the development team, solving the design problems encountered in the development process.

Goals

  • Showcasing the history and culture of Coconut Grove.
  • Introduce landmark historic buildings.
  • Help users discover the treasure locations of the area.
  • Inspire travel to promote local economic development and enrich the residents' lives.
  • Attract Miami tourists to Coconut Grove.
  • Mark the location of each scenic spot to help users develop a tour route.
  • Help users learn about the area and buildings during the tour.
  • Encourage users to ride a bike or walk with family or friends.

Target users

The target user of Know Your Grove is tourists and local residents.

Tourists

Tourists are mostly 20-70 years old, mainly from other states in the United States. Most tourists travel with families or in couple. Most live in Miami Beach or downtown Miami. They want to experience local specials, such as speedboats, diving, kayaking, visiting Little Havana, etc., They want to relax and enjoy time with family.

Local residents

Most residents in Coconut Grove are middle-class and high-income families. Most of them are 35 to 65 years old. Most residents I met in this area are female residents. They like to take a walk around after dinner or rest with their families.

Research

Sample size: 12 participants in total

  • 75% female 25% male
  • 6 participants for user segment A(tourists)
  • 6 participants for user segment B(local residents)

Research Findings

  • Most local residents do not know the historic buildings in the area.
  • Some of the local residents have lived in the area for generations and some are new residents.
  • Local residents are willing to share old photos of historic buildings in the area.
  • Tourists believe that it is more important to relax, they don't want to waste vacation time and spend time where it is worthwhile.
  • Tourists do not want to look at the phone screen while enjoying the sun.
  • Tourists think it may be unsafe to look at a mobile phone while they are riding a bike.

Persona

According to three different target users, I made three Personas, they are: people who have lived here for generations, new residents and tourists.

Competitor Analysis

There are no existing similar apps for Coconut Grove in Miami. I have conducted competitive analysis on similar products used in other locations. I inspired by competitor's interaction design and visual design and applied it into the design of Know Your Grove, I also try to do a better job on interaction and visual design compared with other products.

Conclusion

Most of the competitor display less picture and mainly serves the purpose of information browsing, leading to weak sense of participation for users and users usually reluctant to download additional voice packages. I should add more pictures, reduce text messages, and add audio guide functions into the app. The interface design should be novel and simple, I should also learn from the interaction logic of outstanding competitive products.

Product description

App Know Your Grove will help users find the locations of attractions in Coconut Grove and provide navigation based on the user's location.
There is a map of Coconut Grove in this app. We will mark different attractions on the map. When the user clicks on an attraction, a brief introduction about it will appear at the bottom of the screen. When the user arrives at the selected attraction, the user can scan the QR code to unlock it according to the prompt in the App, users can browse or listen to more information about the attraction and browse photos.
App Know Your Grove will also recommend nearby attractions based on the user's location, displaying unlocked attractions and the number of all attractions at the top. In addition to the map, there is also a list view of the attractions in the App, and users can use the list to select the attraction they are interested in or read attraction information.

Features and Functions

  • Show the user's location on the map.
  • Mark the location of each attraction on the map.
  • Display various attractions in a list.
  • When the user selects an attraction, the user will see the information related to it.
  • Provides navigation routes to selected locations.
  • When a user arrives at an attraction, they can unlock the attraction by scanning the QR code.
  • After unlocking, users can browse more information about the attraction, listen to the voice introduction, and view photos of the attraction.
  • When the user unlocks an attraction, the app shows that it has been unlocked.

Tessler's Law

  • All processes have a core of complexity that cannot be designed away and therefore must be assumed by either the system or the user.
  • Ensure as much as possible of the burden is lifted from users by dealing with inherent complexity during design and development.
  • Take care not to simplify interfaces to the point of abstraction.

Application

  • Keep frequently used information easy to find and hide less frequently used information.
  • The interface content is classified according to the functions.

Application of Interaction Rules

Hick's law

The time it takes for a person to make a decision as a result of the possible choices: increasing the number of choices will increase the decision time logarithmically.

Application

  • Less frequently used functions can be hidden and placed in corners.
  • Split the task flow to make users pay attention to the current selections.
  • Classification of functions allows users to make choices faster and improve interaction efficiency.

Occam's razor

Entities should not be multiplied without necessity, or more simply, the simplest explanation is usually the right one.

Application

  • Focus on displaying main functions and reducing user selections.
  • Keep the frequently used interface simple.
  • Merge processes, press on "Scenic Spots" to directly display the navigation route.

Mobility Usability

Application

  • Place frequently used functions where users can reach them easily.
  • Place infrequent functions in a location that is not easily accessible to prevent accidental touch.
